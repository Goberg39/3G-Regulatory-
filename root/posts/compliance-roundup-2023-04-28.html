April 28, 2023

Compliance Roundup

HMDA Errors; Massachusetts Findings; Washington Enforcement Action

Thanks for tuning in to our summary of the top legal and compliance news from the past week.

Top Stories

HMDA and Data Accuracy Continue to Draw Regulatory Attention

Regulators continue emphasizing the importance of accurate data collection and reporting. As HMDA reporting obligations become increasingly central to fair lending supervision, institutions should expect heightened scrutiny of data integrity controls, validation processes, and correction procedures.

Even relatively small reporting errors can generate examination findings if they reveal weaknesses in an institution's broader compliance management system.

Massachusetts Regulatory Findings

Massachusetts regulators remain active in reviewing consumer compliance programs and mortgage operations. Recent developments continue to reinforce the expectation that institutions maintain effective policies, training programs, and monitoring processes capable of identifying compliance issues before regulators do.

As always, documentation and evidence of monitoring activities are just as important as the control itself when responding to examiners.

Washington Enforcement Action

Washington continues to be one of the more aggressive state regulators in the mortgage industry. Recent enforcement activity serves as another reminder that state-level regulators frequently pursue issues that federal agencies may overlook.

Mortgage companies operating nationally should ensure state-specific requirements are incorporated into their compliance programs rather than relying solely on federal standards.

Examination Takeaways

  • Continue monitoring HMDA accuracy and data integrity.
  • Document compliance testing and corrective actions.
  • Maintain state-specific regulatory awareness.
  • Review examination findings for emerging trends.
  • Ensure training reflects current enforcement priorities.

Other News

  • Regulators continue focusing on fair lending and consumer complaint management.
  • State enforcement activity remains elevated despite lower mortgage volume.
  • Data quality reviews are becoming increasingly important during examinations.

Wells' Woes

This week's Wells Fargo segment highlighted consumer complaint activity and ongoing reputational issues facing the organization.

One statistic stood out above all others: more than 17,000 consumer complaints were reportedly filed against Wells Fargo with the CFPB during the prior twelve-month period.

Sometimes compliance lessons are best learned by watching someone else repeatedly make the same mistakes.

The constant appearance of Wells Fargo within enforcement actions, litigation, consumer complaints, and regulatory scrutiny continues to provide a seemingly endless source of compliance case studies.
