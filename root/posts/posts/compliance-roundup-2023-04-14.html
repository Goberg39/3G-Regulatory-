Compliance Roundup

FDIC Highlights RESPA Violations with Referral Relationships and FCRA Violations Related to Trigger Leads

Thanks for tuning in to our summary of the top legal and compliance news from the past week.

Top Stories

FDIC Highlights RESPA Kickback Issues in Supervisory Report

In its supervisory report looking back on 2022 examination activity, the FDIC highlighted numerous RESPA risks involving referral relationships disguised as lead-generation arrangements.

Examiners identified situations involving warm transfers, preferred lender recommendations, paid ranking systems, and other practices that regulators believed crossed the line from legitimate marketing into prohibited referral activity.

Activities Identified as Potential RESPA Risks

  • Steering consumers toward a single lender.
  • Warm-transfer arrangements.
  • Describing lenders using preferential language.
  • Compensation tied directly to transfers or referrals.
  • Pay-to-play ranking systems presented as neutral comparisons.

Compliance Recommendations

  • Train staff on RESPA requirements.
  • Develop lead-generation policies and procedures.
  • Require annual certifications from loan officers.
  • Conduct monitoring of lead-generation activities.

Trigger Leads and FCRA Compliance

FDIC examiners also focused on institutions purchasing trigger leads but failing to provide the firm offers of credit required by the Fair Credit Reporting Act.

Regulators reviewed recorded calls, scripts, complaints and marketing materials to verify violations and recommended stronger controls over trigger-lead campaigns.

Consumer Complaint Trends

FDIC reported more than 22,000 consumer complaints during 2022, including continued growth in complaints involving vendors, fair lending concerns, disclosures, escrow administration and credit reporting.

CFPB Revises APOR Methodology

CFPB announced changes to the methodology used to calculate the Average Prime Offer Rate following updates to Freddie Mac survey practices.

Other News

  • FFIEC released its 2023 HMDA Guide.
  • HUD expanded access to certain 40-year modification programs.
  • Zillow settled shareholder litigation related to CFPB RESPA investigations.

Wells' Woes

This week's Wells Fargo installment featured allegations regarding sales culture and workplace conduct during the era of the bank's fake-account scandal.
